DJ of the Week, 05-05-2014:

Ben West

DJ of the Bird Nerd Thirty


Give a Brief description of your show: names, dj names, how long have you been doing this, style, etc.
I’m Ben West, Class of 2016 and DJ of the Bird Nerd Thirty. My show is a combination of talk about birds, bird recordings, and a little bit of (human) rock-and-roll. I like to mix the bird songs and calls to emulate certain environments, say the coast of Maine in winter or Bowdoin Campus in spring.

Favorite bird (visually)?
At this point in my life it’s a toss-up between the pileated woodpecker and the sword-billed hummingbird. The former is huge and majestic, while the latter has a crazy-long bill and commanding presence, despite its small size.

Favorite bird song (aurally)?
This is a tough one… I’m going say winter wren. I thought about purple finch, house finch, rose-breasted grosbeak, hermit thrush, and plain-tailed wren, also.

Best response from a listner?
My grandma getting up at 6:30 AM Mountain Time to listen to my show and telling me she really liked the show was pretty great.

Greatest musician of all time and why?
Superb lyrebird. It can basically imitate any sound (chainsaws, other birds, camera shutters, car alarms), and it can make two sounds at once due to dual voice boxes.

Song that makes you cry?
The European starling imitation of an eastern meadowlark song. The former is invasive and the latter is in decline due to habitat loss.

Most amazing concert experience?
ZZ Top at the Jones County Fair in Iowa. Or over spring break when I was surrounded by almost a hundred sparrows and there were owls calling and cardinals singing.

Best song to study to?
Dolphins by Mike Marshall and Darol Anger. It’s instrumental. I find both lyrics and bird songs too distracting.

If you were stuck on a desert island, what three albums would you take with you and why?
Stokes Field Guide to Bird Song Eastern Region, Born to Run by Springsteen, and Let It Be by the Replacements. The Stokes Guide so I could hone my ear birding skills for my hopeful escape, Born to Run for Clarence Clemon’s sweet sax, and Let It Be for some alt-rock.

Best guilty pleasure and/or sing-a-long song and why?
Flashdance…What a Feeling – Irene Cara

If you could meet any musician, dead or alive, who would it be and why?
The Kaua’i o’o. It’s an extinct Hawaiian bird that had a cool, melancholy song that kind of reminds me of an oriole’s song. I really like the idea of seeing an extinct bird.

MFavorite Bowdoin Dining soup?
Armenian apricot lentil.

Favorite place on campus?
Bowdoin Pines (for the birds, of course).

When can we tune into your show?
8:30 AM EST on Fridays!
