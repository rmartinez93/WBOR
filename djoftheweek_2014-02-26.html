DJ of the Week, 02-26-2014:

Itzel Rosales

DJ itzebra


Give a Brief description of your show: names, dj names, how long have you been doing this, style, etc.
My name is Itzel, but you can call me DJ itzebra. I’m a sophomore and this is my second semester having a radio show. I play a variety of Latin American music such as, salsa, bachata, pop, rap, cumbia, you name it. I named the show Roaming around because I play music from different countries like Mexico, Peru, Argentina and Columbia.

Greatest musician of all time and why?
Greatest musician? I’m not sure. But greatest band is definitely Maná. They are a Mexican rock band from Guadalajara, Mexico and they have been together since 1986! That’s right, they have been together for 28 years.

Song that makes you cry?
Say Something by A Great Big World featuring Christina Aguilera definitely tugs at my feelings. I feel like that little boy on a YouTube video who is crying in the backseat of his dad’s car while he listens to the chorus of the song.

Most amazing concert experience?
I went to a 1964: The Tribute concert, it was pretty awesome. The Rolling Stones magazine has named it the “Best Beatles Tribute on Earth.” I remember dancing to the song, “I Saw Her Standing There.” I probably played that song a couple of hundred times after the concert.

Best song to study to?
Anything with a cello. I love the cello, it is not screechy like a violin but it is not as deep as a bass. It is perfect

If you were stuck on a desert island, what three albums would you take with you and why?
Jarabe de Palo-La Flaca
Calle 13-Entren Los Que Quieran
La Santa Cecilia- Treinta Dias

Best guilty pleasure and/or sing-a-long song and why?
Hahahaha, Dark Horse by Katy Perry. It just gets me all pumped.

If you could meet any musician, dead or alive, who would it be and why?
I would like to meet Pedro Infante, he was a famous Mexican actor AND singer in the 1940s and 1950s. He is the love of my life! He died in a plane crash and some conspiracy theorists believe that he is still alive and that he is hiding.

Most played song on your iTunes?
Ice el Hielo by La Santa Cecilia. They are an awesome music group based in Los Angeles, California.

Favorite era of music?
I don’t really have a favorite music era, I enjoy listening to any genre of music except for screamo.

Favorite place on campus?
I really like the second floor of Searles. Especially when sunlight passes through the window glass and the colors of the light are seen.

Favorite vegetable?
Butternut squash, I think it’s a vegetable.

When can we tune into your show?
You can tune in to Roaming Around every Wednesday from 1-2pm.
